# SmartPlay Caddie — Field Guide

*Your phone is the whole caddie.*

One app turns the phone in your pocket into a talking caddie, a swing coach, and a GPS
rangefinder — no extra hardware, no setup ritual. This is the simple guide to using every part
of it.

> **For:** golfers who don't have time to be range rats · **Works on:** iPhone & Android · **Read time:** ~10 min

---

## Start here — 60 seconds

You don't have to set anything up. Open the app and your caddie is already listening.

1. **Open the app.** Tap the **Caddie** tab and just talk — ask a question out loud.
2. **Heading out?** Tap **Play** → pick your course → **Start Round**. Yardages appear automatically.
3. **Practicing?** Open **SwingLab**, prop the phone up, and hit. It films and grades your swing.

---

## 1. Setup & the essentials

There isn't much. The whole idea is that help starts the moment you open the app.

The first time you open SmartPlay Caddie, allow **microphone**, **location**, and **camera**
access when asked. That's what lets the caddie hear you, place you on the course, and film your
swing. You can change any of these later in your phone's settings.

**The five tabs**

- **Caddie** — talk to your AI caddie about anything: club choice, strategy, rules, your own game.
- **Play** — start a round, see the hole, get live yardages.
- **Scorecard** — keep score, track shots, and see who you're playing with.
- **SwingLab** — record and analyze your swing; run drills and tempo work.
- **Dashboard** — your stats, points, and progress over time.

> **Pick your caddie's voice.** In Settings you can choose a personality — **Kevin**, **Serena**,
> **Harry**, or **Tank** — or build a **Custom** caddie that sounds the way you want. It's the same
> brain underneath; only the delivery changes.

## 2. Your Caddie — just talk

The caddie is the heart of the app. You don't tap through menus to get help — you ask.

On the **Caddie** tab, speak naturally. During a round it already knows the hole you're on, the
distance, your recent shots, and your tendencies, so you can ask short questions and get a real
answer.

**Things worth asking**

- *"What club here?"* — a recommendation for the shot in front of you.
- *"How far to carry that bunker?"* — a number, not a guess.
- *"What are we working on?"* — ties your round to your recent practice.
- *"Play a song"* — a kid-safe music portal for the cart.
- *"Take me to SmartMotion"* — the caddie can open screens for you.

> **It's honest by design.** If the caddie doesn't have the data to answer precisely, it tells you
> that instead of inventing a number. When it's estimating, it says so.

**Hands-free on the course.** During a round the caddie can stay in a listening mode so you can ask
without touching the phone. If you'd rather it only speak when asked, just tap the mic when you want
it — it will never talk over you.

## 3. On the course

Start a round and the app quietly becomes your rangefinder, scorecard, and course book.

**Starting a round.** Tap **Play**, search for your course, and choose **Start Round**. The app
pulls the course's public scorecard — par and yardage for every hole — automatically. If it's a
course we've never seen, it still assembles a satellite view and yardages for you (see section 5).

**Live yardages & shot tracking**

- **Front / middle / back** distances to the green update as you walk or drive.
- Mark where your shot came to rest and the app logs the **club and distance**, quietly building
  your real bag yardages over time.
- **Plays-like distance** factors in uphill and downhill so the number matches the shot.

**Scorecard.** Keep score hole by hole. Add the people you're playing with and their handicaps so
the card shows your group. Penalties and putts fold into your stats without extra taps.

> **Spotty signal is fine.** GPS degrades gracefully — if the fix drops, the app keeps working with
> the last known position and honest labels rather than freezing or showing a wrong number.

## 4. SwingLab — practice & swing analysis

Prop your phone up, hit balls, and get a clear read on the swing — at the range, in a net, or in
your garage.

- **SmartMotion** — films your swing and overlays your body motion, tempo, and the clubhead path.
  It only shows a metric it can actually see — no fabricated spin or launch numbers.
- **Cage Mode** — hitting into a net where you can't see ball flight? Cage Mode listens to the
  strike acoustically and analyzes swing after swing in one session.
- **Tempo Trainer** — dials in your rhythm, the ratio between your backswing and downswing — the
  single biggest lever for consistent contact.
- **Drills & Coach Mode** — goal-driven drills open SmartMotion already set up to watch the one
  thing that drill is about, then judge whether you did it.

**Honest contact.** A thin or fat strike is never dressed up as a good swing. Green means a
confirmed, clean strike — nothing else. That honesty is the point: you can trust what the analysis
tells you.

**Your swing library.** Every recorded swing is saved so you can scrub through it in slow motion,
compare against an earlier version of yourself, and see the clubhead arc traced and color-coded by
speed. Uploaded clips get the same treatment.

> **Left-handed?** The overlays and guides mirror automatically, and each family member's
> handedness is remembered.

## 5. SmartVision & SmartFinder

See the hole from above, and turn your camera into a rangefinder.

**SmartVision — the hole from above.** SmartVision shows the hole you're playing as an aerial view
with the green, tee, and hazards marked. One toggle in the corner switches between two views:

- **Satellite** — a live aerial of the actual hole. This now works on **any** course, even one
  we've never pre-loaded, by assembling the imagery and reading the green straight off the satellite.
- **Static** — a hand-curated hole photo, where one exists.

> **When you see "AI ESTIMATE,"** it means the green and tee were read from satellite imagery rather
> than surveyed data. It's a smart starting point, clearly labeled so you always know what you're
> looking at — and it never overrides real course data.

**SmartFinder — point and measure.** Aim your camera at a target — a bunker, a tree, a spot on the
fairway — and SmartFinder reads the distance to it. On the course it uses your GPS position; off the
course it works as a standalone measuring tool, perfect for stepping off distances on the range.

## 6. Dashboard & progress

Where practice turns into lower scores — and you can see the connection.

The **Dashboard** gathers your rounds, your practice, and your swing work into one place. You earn
**points** for practicing, and the dashboard shows how that practice lines up with your scoring over
time. It's careful to describe that as an association, not a promise — real numbers, honestly framed.

- **Your bag** — real, measured yardages per club, editable by you.
- **Fit profile** — a directional read on your equipment from your actual distances and tempo.
- **Streaks & points** — a nudge to keep showing up.

## 7. Bring your own gear

Already own some tech? The app plugs into it — no new purchases required.

- **Ray-Ban Meta glasses** — on Android, connect your glasses so the caddie can see what you see for
  richer, hands-free reads. Turn it on in Settings.
- **Galaxy Watch** — capture swing motion from your wrist and glance at yardages without pulling out
  your phone.
- **Import past rounds** — snap a photo of a scorecard, or import from another app, and your history
  comes along, including a starting handicap.
- **SmartPump workouts** — import a golf-fitness workout (PDF, CSV, or JSON) and see how your
  training tracks against your performance.

> **Photo imports are forgiving.** Scorecards, Toptracer screenshots, club photos — if a photo can't
> be read clearly, the app says so in plain language instead of throwing an error.

## 8. Good to know

- **It never fakes a number.** Estimates are labeled, missing data is admitted, and a bad strike is
  never shown as a good one. Trust is the whole product.
- **It keeps working offline.** Course books, yardages, and your data are cached on the phone, so a
  dead signal doesn't stop the round.
- **Your data is yours.** You can back it up and restore it, and nothing about your game leaves
  without you.
- **Zero setup is the point.** Any time the app makes you tap through a wall before it helps, that's
  a bug — tell us.

## 9. Troubleshooting

| If… | Do this |
|---|---|
| The caddie didn't answer my first question | Give it a second on a fresh open while it wakes up, then ask again. Make sure microphone access is allowed. |
| No yardages on the course | Confirm location access is on and you've tapped Start Round. Yardages fill in within a few seconds of a GPS fix. |
| SmartVision shows a wide, zoomed-out view | That's the fallback for a course with no detailed geometry yet. Switch the toggle to Satellite — it centers on your position. |
| My swing didn't get a skeleton overlay | Make sure your whole body is in frame and the phone is steady. Down-the-line (behind you) framing gives the cleanest read. |
| A photo import failed | Retake it in good light with the whole card or screen in frame. The app tells you if it still can't read it. |

# Persona: James — The Returning Golfer

## Profile
- **Name**: James
- **Age**: 47
- **Handicap**: 18 (was 12 ten years ago)
- **Plays**: Just getting back, 1-2 rounds/month, range occasionally
- **Goals**: Get back to enjoying the game, rebuild confidence
- **Pain points**: Frustrated by current level vs memory of better, body
  changed, swing changed
- **Tech comfort**: Moderate — rusty on golf apps, was an early adopter back when
- **Voice with Kevin**: All three modes; wants supportive coaching feel;
  **Psychologist register matters**

## Mental model
James downloads SmartPlay because returning golfers need help. Wants
onboarding to feel **welcoming, not overwhelming**. Wants Kevin to feel like
a real caddie helping him **rebuild**. Will use cage occasionally, plays
rounds casually. Easily discouraged by frustration. Needs encouragement
that's **genuine, not phony**.

## Critical needs from product
- **Onboarding feels welcoming**, paced for someone re-learning
- **Kevin's Psychologist register lands** — encouragement that doesn't feel cheap
- **Cage analysis hedges appropriately** (his swing is rusty; low confidence
  is honest, not failure)
- **Drills accessible without intimidation**
- **Recap acknowledges progress** even on rough rounds
- **Course difficulty awareness** (Kevin doesn't recommend pro shots)
- **No relentless decline-from-past framing**

## Failure modes that lose James
- Onboarding feels like a tech tutorial, not a welcome
- Kevin too tactical when he needs encouragement
- Cage analysis assumes he's better than he is (pro-level critique on
  beginner-level swing)
- Stats relentlessly show his decline from past
- Recap doesn't acknowledge effort/progress
- Kevin pushes him toward "fix this" before he's ready
- Drill library feels like graduate-level reading
